## **Purpose**

This assignment will ask you to connect with and work with students in the Art class, and to document your plans to achieve the goals set forth in "[Assignment 3 - ART 120 Collaboration - TinkerCAD design](./assignment-3-art-120-collaboration-tinkercad-design.md "Assignment 3 - ART 120 Collaboration - TinkerCAD design")"

## **Tasks**

**NOTE: You must limit yourself to the topics and techniques we have covered in this class (or a pre-req class), and within this class you can only use those topics that we were covered when this assignment was first due.**

During week 5 of the quarter you must work with the Art students to define what you all think the project should be. The Art students will be responsible for making sure that their 3D printed design meets the requirements for their course and you all need to make sure that the final project meets the requirements listed in "[Assignment 3 - ART 120 Collaboration - TinkerCAD design](./assignment-3-art-120-collaboration-tinkercad-design.md "Assignment 3 - ART 120 Collaboration - TinkerCAD design")"

For this assignment you must do three things:

1.  You must work with the entire team (\"team\" being both the IT-CS 115 students and the ART 120 students) and figure out what you\'ll be doing for the project.
    -   This is the most important part of this whole assignment;  the rest of it is just so the teacher can double-check that you\'re doing this part.\
        \
2.  You must document your plan for meeting the requirements listed in "[Assignment 3 - ART 120 Collaboration - TinkerCAD design](./assignment-3-art-120-collaboration-tinkercad-design.md "Assignment 3 - ART 120 Collaboration - TinkerCAD design")"  For this part you should create a document (Word or PDF - it needs to be something that you can hand in here to Canvas) that describes what you\'ll be making and how it fulfills all the requirements.  You\'re encouraged to include a diagram / picture of some sort, although it doesn\'t need to include all the details (for example, a hand-drawn picture of which LEDs will be connected to the Arduino would be very helpful; you don\'t need to worry about drawing in all the resistors nor the exact pins that you\'ll connect each wire to, etc)\
    \
3.  You must document all your team interactions using a table like the following example: (feel free to copy and paste it into your document):
