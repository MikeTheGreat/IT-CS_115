## Why We're Doing This {#why-were-doing-this data-start="388" end="413"}

If you are new to college, new to using a laptop for school, or feel nervous about technology, you are **not alone**. Many students arrive at college without being shown how to set up their computer or organize digital files---and no one should be expected to already know this.

This assignment is designed to help you get set up **step by step**, with support available along the way. It is **not a test**, and it is okay if things don't work perfectly the first time.

The goal is to reduce stress later in the quarter by making sure your technology works *now*.

Cascadia provides free access to Microsoft tools like Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and OneDrive.

### Downloading Microsoft 365 {#downloading-microsoft-365 data-start="2173" end="2193"}

[Instructions](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/download-install-or-reinstall-microsoft-365-or-office-2024-on-a-pc-or-mac-4414eaaf-0478-48be-9c42-23adc4716658){.inline_disabled target="_blank"}

### Alternative: Bookmark Microsoft 365 in Your Browser {#alternative-bookmark-microsoft-365-in-your-browser data-start="2348" end="2400"}

If your computer cannot install Microsoft apps, you will use the web version. Bookmarking ensures you can always find it.

**Step 1: Open Microsoft Word in the Browser**

1.  Go to [Microsoft 365](https://myapps.microsoft.com/){.inline_disabled target="_blank"}.
2.  Log in with your Cascadia account.

**Step 2: Bookmark the Page**

1.  Look at the **address bar** at the top of your browser.\
    Click the **star ⭐ icon** (usually on the right side).
2.  Name the bookmark something clear, such as:
    -   `Microsoft 365`{style="font-size: 1rem;" data-start="2916" end="2931"}
3.  Save it to your **Bookmarks Bar**[ or an easy-to-find folder.]{style="font-family: inherit; font-size: 1rem;"}

✅ **You're done when:** You can click the bookmark to and open OneDrive anytime.

## Task 3: Set Up Your OneDrive Folder Organization {#task-3-set-up-your-onedrive-folder-organization data-start="3117" end="3170"}

Having a clear folder system helps you avoid losing work and reduces last-minute stress.

1.  Open **OneDrive** using your Cascadia Microsoft account (either on the desktop or the browser).
2.  [Create a ]{style="font-family: inherit; font-size: 1rem;"}**main folder**[ called:]{style="font-family: inherit; font-size: 1rem;"}
    -   `Winter 2026 Classes`{data-start="3369" end="3390"}
3.  Inside this folder:
    -   Create **one folder for each class** you are taking.
4.  Inside each class folder, create smaller folders by lecture or assignment type, such as:
    -   `01 Lecture`{data-start="3574" end="3582"}, `02 Lecture`{data-start="3584" end="3592"}, ... OR `Notes`{data-start="3599" end="3606"}, `Homework`{data-start="3608" end="3618"}, `Projects`{data-start="3620" end="3630"}

There is no single "correct" system---what matters is that **you can find your work later**.

✅ **You're done when:** You can clearly see folders for each class with organized subfolders inside them.

## Important File-Saving Reminder {#important-file-saving-reminder data-start="3842" end="3877"}

⚠️ **Do NOT work out of your Downloads folder.**

Many students lose work because files are left in Downloads. To prevent this:

-   After downloading a file, **move or copy it immediately** into the correct OneDrive folder.
-   Always **open and edit files from OneDrive**, not Downloads.

This ensures your work:

-   Is automatically saved
-   Won't be accidentally overwritten
-   Can be accessed from any device
-   Will be easier to submit correctly

## How to Take Screenshots {#how-to-take-screenshots data-start="4346" end="4374"}

You will need screenshots to complete this assignment. Follow the instructions for your computer below.

### 💻 Windows {#windows data-start="4483" end="4499"}

**Option 1: Part of the Screen (Recommended)**

1.  Press **Windows + Shift + S**.
2.  Drag to select the area you want to capture.
3.  The screenshot is copied to your clipboard.
4.  Navigate to your **OneDrive folder**, right-click → **Paste**, or press **Ctrl + V** to save it as an image file.

 

**Option 2: Full Screen**

1.  Press **Windows + Print Screen (PrtScn)**.
2.  Your screen will dim briefly.
3.  The screenshot is automatically saved as a **PNG file** in:\
    `C:\Users\[Your Name]\Pictures\Screenshots`{data-start="645" end="688"}
4.  **Move the file** into your **OneDrive folder** for the class.

### 🍎 Mac {#mac data-start="4861" end="4873"}

**Option 1: Part of Screen (Recommended)**

1.  Press **Command (⌘) + Shift + 4**.\]
2.  Drag to select area.
3.  Screenshot automatically saves to Desktop.
4.  Move it to **OneDrive**.

**Option 2: Full Screen**

1.  Press **Command (⌘) + Shift + 3**.
2.  Screenshot saves to Desktop.
3.  Move it to **OneDrive**.

### Put your screenshots into a single .PDF file
As you take each screenshot please paste it into a document (Word Doc, Google Doc, Apple Pages, etc)
and write a brief caption/explanation beneath the image explaining what the image is.  (Normally it's 
pretty clear what the screenshot is but if it's not then the caption will help your teacher understand
what you were trying to do.)

When you go to upload your file you should save your document in .PDF format.  Look for a "File > Export" or "File > Save As" menu option to do this.
