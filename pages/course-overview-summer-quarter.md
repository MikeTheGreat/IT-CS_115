#### Coding Exercises {#coding-exercises heading="Weekly Exercises"}

- Note: 115 will also do \"coding related exercises\", such as finding and fixing errors, tracing programs, etc
- Coding exercises involve straightforward tasks (e.g., using an array).
- Coding exercises designed to prepare students for larger assignments.
- Completion graded, similar to labor-based grading.
  - Focus on overall effort rather than perfection.
  - Encourages attempting all exercises without detailed correctness checks.
    - I will look at a subset of the exercises each week and provide feedback
- But! Make sure that your code compiles, runs, and includes all the required work

  - Make sure that you run your code from main() to check that it works
- You can keep handing in your work for the Coding Exercises until I finish grading it (without penalty)
- Once I finish grading it then you either get a grade or a zero (if you didn\'t hand in any work)

### Time Management Recommendations {#time-management-recommendations heading="Time Management Recommendations"}

- I encourage y\'all to do thorough planning at the beginning of the quarter to avoid time management issues later.
- Check the Calendar regularly
  - Review due dates and plan ahead considering personal schedules.
- Course Time Commitment:
  - Cascadia's recommendation: 2-3 hours per credit each week for a normal 11-week quarter.
  - For a five-credit course, this translates to 10-15 hours per week
  - The total workload is approximately (10-15 hours/week) times (11 weeks) = 110 hours to 165 hours
  - Weekly summer workload is therefore (total workload / 8 = ) 13.75 hours to 20.6 hours per week
- It\'s best to overestimate Study Time:
  - I suggest overestimating the time needed (ex: budgeting 25 hours) and adjusting if less time is required.
  - It\'s easier to reduce scheduled time over trying to find additional hours later.
- Schedule Planning:
  - I recommend creating a daily schedule, hour by hour, including all activities (sleep, meals, work, personal time, etc.).
  - Make sure to account for travel time, family responsibilities, and other obligations.
